## 1. Number Series & Coding

1. Find the next number in the series: 1, 9, 25, 49, 81, ?
A) 100
B) 112
C) 121
D) 144

**Answer:** Option **C**

**Explanation:**
The given series consists of squares of consecutive odd numbers.
1^2 = 1
3^2 = 9
5^2 = 25
7^2 = 49
9^2 = 81
Next number should be 11^2.
11^2 = 121.

2. Find the missing number: 2, 5, 9, 19, 37, ?
A) 73
B) 75
C) 76
D) 78

**Answer:** Option **B**

**Explanation:**
The pattern alternates between multiplying by 2 and adding 1, and multiplying by 2 and subtracting 1.
2 x 2 + 1 = 5
5 x 2 - 1 = 9
9 x 2 + 1 = 19
19 x 2 - 1 = 37
Next number should be 37 x 2 + 1.
37 x 2 + 1 = 74 + 1 = 75.

3. If in a certain language, MADRAS is coded as NBESBT, how is BOMBAY coded in that code?
A) CPNCBX
B) CPNCBZ
C) CPOCBZ
D) CQOCBZ

**Answer:** Option **B**

**Explanation:**
Each letter in the word is moved one step forward to obtain the corresponding letter of the code.
M -> N
A -> B
D -> E
R -> S
A -> B
S -> T
Similarly, for BOMBAY:
B -> C
O -> P
M -> N
B -> C
A -> B
Y -> Z
So, BOMBAY is coded as CPNCBZ.

4. If Z = 52 and ACT = 48, then BAT will be equal to:
A) 39
B) 41
C) 44
D) 46

**Answer:** Option **D**

**Explanation:**
In the English alphabet, Z is the 26th letter.
Z = 26 x 2 = 52.
A = 1, C = 3, T = 20.
ACT = (1 + 3 + 20) x 2 = 24 x 2 = 48.
Similarly, for BAT:
B = 2, A = 1, T = 20.
BAT = (2 + 1 + 20) x 2 = 23 x 2 = 46.

5. In a certain code, '15789' is written as EGKPT and '2346' is written as ALUR. How is '23549' written in that code?
A) ALEUT
B) ALGTU
C) ALGUT
D) ALGRT

**Answer:** Option **C**

**Explanation:**
The letters are assigned as direct codes to the digits:
1 = E, 5 = G, 7 = K, 8 = P, 9 = T
2 = A, 3 = L, 4 = U, 6 = R
Therefore, for 23549:
2 -> A
3 -> L
5 -> G
4 -> U
9 -> T
So, 23549 is written as ALGUT.

## 2. Blood Relations & Direction Sense

1. Pointing to a photograph of a boy Suresh said, "He is the son of the only son of my mother." How is Suresh related to that boy?
A) Brother
B) Uncle
C) Cousin
D) Father

**Answer:** Option **D**

**Explanation:**
The "only son of my mother" is Suresh himself.
So, the statement becomes: "He is the son of Suresh."
Therefore, Suresh is the father of the boy.

2. A man said to a lady, "Your mother's husband's sister is my aunt." How is the lady related to the man?
A) Daughter
B) Granddaughter
C) Mother
D) Sister

**Answer:** Option **D**

**Explanation:**
Lady's mother's husband = Lady's father.
Lady's father's sister = Lady's aunt.
So, Lady's aunt is man's aunt.
Therefore, the lady and the man are siblings. The lady is the sister of the man.

3. A man walks 5 km toward south and then turns to the right. After walking 3 km he turns to the left and walks 5 km. Now in which direction is he from the starting place?
A) West
B) South
C) North-East
D) South-West

**Answer:** Option **D**

**Explanation:**
The man starts at a point, walks 5 km South.
He turns right (which is West) and walks 3 km.
He turns left (which is South) and walks 5 km.
Total distance South from start = 5 km + 5 km = 10 km.
Total distance West from start = 3 km.
Since he is both South and West of the starting point, his direction is South-West.

4. Rahul put his timepiece on the table in such a way that at 6 P.M. hour hand points to North. In which direction the minute hand will point at 9.15 P.M.?
A) South-East
B) South
C) North
D) West

**Answer:** Option **D**

**Explanation:**
Normally, at 6 P.M., the hour hand points South.
If it points North, the entire clock is rotated by 180 degrees.
Normally, at 9:15 P.M., the minute hand points East (at the number 3).
If we rotate the clock by 180 degrees, East becomes West.
So, the minute hand will point West.

5. P, Q, R, S, T, U, V and W are sitting round the circle and are facing the centre: P is second to the right of T who is the neighbour of R and V. S is not the neighbour of P. V is the neighbour of U. Q is not between S and W. W is not between U and S. Which two of the following are not neighbours?
A) RV
B) UV
C) RP
D) QW

**Answer:** Option **A**

**Explanation:**
1. T is the neighbor of R and V: Arrangement is R-T-V or V-T-R.
2. P is second to the right of T: Assuming anti-clockwise is right, arrangement must be R-T-V-blank-P.
3. V is the neighbor of U: The blank must be U. Arrangement is R-T-V-U-P.
4. Using the other constraints, we can place S, W, and Q.
The clockwise seating sequence comes out as: R, T, V, U, W, S, Q, P.
Looking at the sequence, R and V are separated by T. They are not neighbors.

## 3. Syllogisms

1. Statements: All dogs are cats. Some cats are rats.
Conclusions: I. Some dogs are rats. II. No dog is rat.
A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** Option **C**

**Explanation:**
All dogs are inside the cats circle.
Some cats overlap with the rats circle.
The rats circle may or may not overlap with the dogs circle.
If it overlaps, I is true and II is false.
If it does not overlap, II is true and I is false.
Since exactly one must be true, the answer is Either I or II follows.

2. Statements: Some buildings are chalks. No chalk is toffee.
Conclusions: I. No building is toffee. II. All chalks are buildings.
A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** Option **D**

**Explanation:**
Some buildings overlap with chalks.
No chalk overlaps with toffee.
Conclusion I: Toffees could overlap with the buildings that are not chalks, so this is not necessarily true.
Conclusion II: Only some buildings are chalks, which does not mean all chalks are buildings.
So, neither conclusion follows.

3. Statements: All windows are doors. No door is a wall.
Conclusions: I. Some windows are walls. II. No wall is a door.
A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** Option **B**

**Explanation:**
All windows are inside the doors circle.
The walls circle is completely separate from the doors circle.
Conclusion I: Since walls cannot touch doors, they definitely cannot touch windows. I is false.
Conclusion II: "No door is a wall" implies directly that "No wall is a door". II is true.
So, only conclusion II follows.

4. Statements: Some papers are pens. All the pencils are pens.
Conclusions: I. Some pens are pencils. II. Some pens are papers.
A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Both I and II follow
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** Option **C**

**Explanation:**
All pencils are pens implies that the set of pencils is a subset of the set of pens.
Conclusion I: Therefore, some pens are definitely pencils. I is true.
Conclusion II: "Some papers are pens" directly implies "Some pens are papers". II is true.
So, both I and II follow.

5. Statements: All buses are cars. All ships are cars.
Conclusions: I. All cars are ships. II. All buses are ships.
A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** Option **D**

**Explanation:**
Buses are inside cars. Ships are inside cars.
Conclusion I: Cars is the larger set, so all cars are not ships. I is false.
Conclusion II: Buses and ships are both inside cars, but they may not overlap at all. II is not necessarily true.
So, neither I nor II follows.
