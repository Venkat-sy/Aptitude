# Module 1: Arithmetic

## 1. Percentages
**Q1. Two students appeared at an examination. One secured 9 marks more than the other and his marks was 56% of the sum of their marks. Marks are:**
*A. 39,30* | *B. 41,32* | *C. 42,33* | *D. 43,34*
**Sol:** $x+9 = 0.56(2x+9) \Rightarrow 25(x+9)=14(2x+9) \Rightarrow 3x=99 \Rightarrow x=33$. Marks: 33, 42.
**Trick:** Check options. Option C sum = 75. $56\%$ of $75 = 42$. Matches. (Ans: C)

**Q2. If 20% of a = b, then b% of 20 is:**
*A. 4% of a* | *B. 5% of a* | *C. 20% of a*
**Sol:** $b = 0.2a$. Req: $b\%$ of $20 = (0.2a/100)*20 = 0.04a = 4\%$ of $a$. (Ans: A)

**Q3. Population increased from 1,75,000 to 2,62,500 in a decade. Average percent increase per year is:**
*A. 4.37%* | *B. 5%* | *C. 6%*
**Sol:** Increase = $87,500$. $\%$ inc = $(87500/175000)*100 = 50\%$. Per yr = $50\%/10 = 5\%$. (Ans: B)

**Q4. A fruit seller sells 40% apples and still has 420. Originally, he had:**
*A. 588* | *B. 600* | *C. 672* | *D. 700*
**Sol:** Left = $60\%$. $0.6x = 420 \Rightarrow x = 700$. (Ans: D)

**Q5. Election between two: one got 55% valid votes. 20% votes invalid. Total votes = 7500. Valid votes for other candidate:**
*A. 2700* | *B. 2900* | *C. 3000*
**Sol:** Valid = $80\%$ of $7500 = 6000$. Other got $45\%$ of Valid. $0.45 * 6000 = 2700$. (Ans: A)

## 2. Profit & Loss
**Q1. CP of 20 articles = SP of x articles. Profit = 25%. x = ?**
*A. 15* | *B. 16* | *C. 18*
**Sol:** $CP=1 \Rightarrow SP(x)=20$. Profit% = $((20-x)/x)*100 = 25 \Rightarrow 1/4 = (20-x)/x \Rightarrow 5x=80 \Rightarrow x=16$. (Ans: B)

**Q2. Vendor bought toffees at 6 for Re 1. How many for Re 1 must he sell to gain 20%?**
*A. 3* | *B. 4* | *C. 5*
**Sol:** $CP = 1/6$. req $SP = (1/6)*1.20 = 1/5$. Sells 5 for Re 1. (Ans: C)

**Q3. Buys cycle for Rs. 1400, sells at 15% loss. SP = ?**
*A. 1090* | *B. 1160* | *C. 1190*
**Sol:** $SP = 0.85 * 1400 = 1190$. (Ans: C)

**Q4. Bought at 6 for Rs. 5, sold at 5 for Rs. 6. Gain % = ?**
*A. 30%* | *B. 33.3%* | *C. 44%*
**Sol:** Total CP = $5*5=25$. Total SP = $6*6=36$. Gain = $36-25=11$. Gain% = $(11/25)*100 = 44\%$. (Ans: C)

**Q5. Selling at 800 -> 25% profit. SP for 25% loss = ?**
*A. 720* | *B. 640* | *C. 480*
**Sol:** $1.25(CP) = 800 \Rightarrow CP = 640$. Req $SP = 0.75 * 640 = 480$. (Ans: C)

## 3. Ratio & Proportion
**Q1. Two numbers are 20% and 50% more than a third. Ratio of the two numbers:**
*A. 2:5* | *B. 3:5* | *C. 4:5*
**Sol:** Numbers: $120, 150$. Ratio = $120:150 = 4:5$. (Ans: C)

**Q2. Sum distributed among A,B,C,D as 5:2:4:3. C gets 1000 more than D. B's share = ?**
*A. 500* | *B. 1500* | *C. 2000*
**Sol:** $C-D = 4x-3x = x = 1000$. $B = 2x = 2000$. (Ans: C)

**Q3. Seats in ratio 5:7:8. Increased by 40%, 50%, 75%. New ratio:**
*A. 2:3:4* | *B. 6:7:8* | *C. 2:3:5*
**Sol:** $50*1.4 : 70*1.5 : 80*1.75 \Rightarrow 70 : 105 : 140 \Rightarrow 2:3:4$. (Ans: A)

**Q4. 60L mixture, Milk:Water = 2:1. To make ratio 1:2, water to add:**
*A. 20* | *B. 30* | *C. 60*
**Sol:** Milk=$40$, Water=$20$. $40/(20+x) = 1/2 \Rightarrow 80 = 20+x \Rightarrow x = 60$. (Ans: C)

**Q5. Fourth proportional to 5, 8, 15 is:**
*A. 18* | *B. 24* | *C. 19*
**Sol:** $5/8 = 15/x \Rightarrow 5x = 120 \Rightarrow x = 24$. (Ans: B)

## 4. SI & CI
**Q1. SI amounts to Rs 815 in 3 yrs, 854 in 4 yrs. Sum = ?**
*A. 650* | *B. 690* | *C. 698*
**Sol:** $SI(1yr) = 854-815=39$. $SI(3yr) = 117$. Sum = $815-117=698$. (Ans: C)

**Q2. Rate % per annum for 1200 to become 1348.32 in 2 yrs CI?**
*A. 6%* | *B. 6.5%* | *C. 7%*
**Sol:** $1200(1+R/100)^2 = 1348.32 \Rightarrow (1+R/100)^2 = 1.1236 = 1.06^2 \Rightarrow R=6$. (Ans: A)

**Q3. SI for 2.5 yrs @ 12% is Rs 40 less than SI for 3.5 yrs @ 10%. Sum = ?**
*A. 600* | *B. 666* | *C. 800*
**Sol:** $P(0.35) - P(0.30) = 40 \Rightarrow 0.05P = 40 \Rightarrow P=800$. (Ans: C)

**Q4. 60% increase in 6 yrs at SI. CI of 12000 after 3 yrs at same rate?**
*A. 2160* | *B. 3120* | *C. 3972*
**Sol:** $R = 60/6 = 10\%$. $Amount = 12000 * 1.1^3 = 15972$. $CI = 3972$. (Ans: C)

**Q5. Diff between CI and SI on sum for 2 yrs @ 4% is Re 1. Sum = ?**
*A. 625* | *B. 630* | *C. 640*
**Sol:** $D = P(R/100)^2 \Rightarrow 1 = P(4/100)^2 \Rightarrow P = 625$. (Ans: A)

## 5. Averages & Ages
**Q1. Avg of 20 numbers is 0. At most how many > 0?**
*A. 0* | *B. 1* | *C. 19*
**Sol:** 19 can be positive, balanced by 1 massive negative number. (Ans: C)

**Q2. Captain=26, Keeper=29. Team avg=x. Exclude them, avg=x-1. Team avg = ?**
*A. 23* | *B. 24* | *C. 25*
**Sol:** $(11x - 55)/9 = x-1 \Rightarrow 11x-55 = 9x-9 \Rightarrow 2x=46 \Rightarrow x=23$. (Ans: A)

**Q3. F+S=60. 6 yrs ago, F-6 = 5(S-6). S in 6 yrs = ?**
*A. 12* | *B. 14* | *C. 20*
**Sol:** $(60-S-6) = 5S-30 \Rightarrow 54-S = 5S-30 \Rightarrow 6S=84 \Rightarrow S=14$. S in 6 yrs = 20. (Ans: C)

**Q4. M = S+24. In 2 yrs, M+2 = 2(S+2). S = ?**
*A. 14* | *B. 22* | *C. 24*
**Sol:** $(S+24)+2 = 2S+4 \Rightarrow S+26 = 2S+4 \Rightarrow S=22$. (Ans: B)

**Q5. Avg H,W,C 3 yrs ago=27. Avg W,C 5 yrs ago=20. Present H = ?**
*A. 35* | *B. 40* | *C. 50*
**Sol:** Present H+W+C = $(27*3)+9 = 90$. Present W+C = $(20*2)+10 = 50$. H = 40. (Ans: B)
