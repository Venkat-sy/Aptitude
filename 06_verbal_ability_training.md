## 1. Sentence Correction & Grammar

1. Read each sentence to find out whether there is any grammatical error in it. The error, if any will be in one part of the sentence. The letter of that part is the answer. If there is no error, the answer is 'D'.

A) One of the boys

B) who always give

C) the correct answer is Samuel

D) No error

**Answer:** Option **D**

**Explanation:**

The sentence follows the structure: "One of the + Plural Noun + who/which/that".

In this structure, the relative pronoun ("who") refers back to the immediate antecedent (the Plural Noun, which is "boys").

Since "who" refers to "boys" (plural), the verb must be plural. The verb "give" is plural.

The main clause "One... is Samuel" is also correct.

There is no grammatical error.

2. Find the error:

A) The sceneries

B) of Kashmir

C) is very charming

D) No error

**Answer:** Option **A**

**Explanation:**

Words like scenery, furniture, hair, information, etc., are uncountable nouns in English.

Uncountable nouns do not have a plural form.

Therefore, "sceneries" should be replaced with "scenery".

---

3. Find the error:

A) I have been

B) working on this project

C) since three years

D) No error

**Answer:** Option **C**

**Explanation:**

"Since" is used to refer to a specific point in time (e.g., since Monday, since 2015).

"For" is used to refer to a duration or period of time.

"Three years" is a duration.

Therefore, "since" should be replaced with "for".

4. Choose the correctly spelt word out of the given alternatives.

A) Ommission

B) Omission

C) Ommision

D) Omision

**Answer:** Option **B**

**Explanation:**

The root word is "Omit". When adding the suffix "-ion", the 't' becomes a double 's', but the 'm' remains single.

The correct spelling is Omission.

5. Choose the word which is the exact OPPOSITE of the given words: OBSCURE

A) Pedantic

B) Implicit

C) Explicit

D) Obnoxious

**Answer:** Option **C**

**Explanation:**

"Obscure" means not discovered or known about; uncertain or hidden.

"Explicit" means stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion or doubt.

Therefore, Explicit is the exact opposite of Obscure.

## 2. Para Jumbles

1. Rearrange the following sentences to form a coherent paragraph.
P. He was so busy with them that he did not get time to eat.
Q. Thousands of people came to him and asked different types of questions.
R. Swami Vivekananda once stayed in a small village.
S. This continued for three days.

A) RQPS

B) SQPR

C) PQRS

D) RSQP

**Answer:** Option **A**

**Explanation:**

Sentence R introduces the main character and setting, making it the first sentence.

Sentence Q describes what happened in the village, following R.

Sentence P explains the effect of the people's questions on him ("them" refers to the people in Q).

Sentence S concludes by giving the duration of the entire event.

The correct sequence is R - Q - P - S.

---

2. Rearrange the following sentences.
A. But he did not know how to find one at that hour.
B. It was his first visit to the city and he didn't know where to go.
C. Mohan decided to take a taxi.
D. The train arrived at the station at midnight.

A) BCDA

B) DBCA

C) CDBA

D) DABC

**Answer:** Option **B**

**Explanation:**

Sentence D is the starting event (arriving at the station).

Sentence B explains his situation upon arriving.

Sentence C is his decision based on the situation.

Sentence A introduces a hurdle to his decision ("But he did not know...").

The correct sequence is D - B - C - A.

3. Rearrange the following sentences.
1. A dog stole a piece of meat from a butcher's shop.
P. He looked down and saw his own reflection.
Q. On his way home, he had to cross a bridge over a stream.
R. He thought it was another dog with another piece of meat.
S. He barked at the reflection and his meat fell into the stream.
6. He lost his meat and had to go home hungry.

A) QPRS

B) PQRS

C) RSPQ

D) SQPR

**Answer:** Option **A**

**Explanation:**

Sentence 1 is given as the first sentence.

Sentence Q describes his journey immediately after stealing.

Sentence P happens when he is on the bridge mentioned in Q.

Sentence R is his thought process after looking down in P.

Sentence S is his reaction to his thought in R.

Sentence 6 is the conclusion given.

The correct sequence is Q - P - R - S.
