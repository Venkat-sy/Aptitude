# Training Module 6: Verbal Ability

## 1. Sentence Correction & Grammar

**Q1. Find the error: One of the boys (A) / who always give (B) / the correct answer (C) / is Samuel. (D)**
> **Solution:** The phrase "One of the boys" acts as the subject for the main verb ("is"). However, the relative clause "who always give" refers to "the boys". "Boys" is plural, so "give" is correct. Wait, the actual IndiaBix rule says: "One of the + Plural Noun + who/which/that + Plural Verb". So "give" is correct.
> The sentence has NO ERROR.
> *(Trick: Many students wrongly change 'give' to 'gives', but because 'who' refers to the plural 'boys', it must remain plural).*

**Q2. Find the error: The sceneries (A) / of Kashmir (B) / is very charming. (C) / No error (D)**
> **Solution:** "Scenery" is an uncountable noun. It does not have a plural form like "sceneries".
> Error is in part (A). It should be "The scenery".

**Q3. Find the error: I have been (A) / working on this project (B) / since three years. (C) / No error (D)**
> **Solution:** "Since" is used for a point in time (since 2010, since Monday). "For" is used for a duration (for three years, for a month).
> Error is in part (C). It should be "for three years".

**Q4. Choose the correctly spelt word:**
*A. Ommission*
*B. Omission*
*C. Ommision*
*D. Omision*
> **Solution:** The correct spelling is B. Omission. (One 'm', double 's').

**Q5. The antonym of 'OBSCURE' is:**
*A. Pedantic*
*B. Implicit*
*C. Explicit*
*D. Obnoxious*
> **Solution:** Obscure means hidden or unclear. The opposite is Explicit (clear, spelled out).

## 2. Para Jumbles

**Q1. Rearrange the sentences to form a coherent paragraph:**
*P. He was so busy with them that he did not get time to eat.*
*Q. Thousands of people came to him and asked different types of questions.*
*R. Swami Vivekananda once stayed in a small village.*
*S. This continued for three days.*
> **Solution:** R introduces the setting and character (Swami Vivekananda).
> Q explains what happened in the village (People came).
> P describes his reaction/state to Q (He was so busy with 'them').
> S concludes the duration of the event.
> Order: R Q P S.

**Q2. Rearrange the sentences:**
*A. But he did not know how to find one at that hour.*
*B. It was his first visit to the city and he didn't know where to go.*
*C. Mohan decided to take a taxi.*
*D. The train arrived at the station at midnight.*
> **Solution:** D sets the scene (arrived at midnight).
> B explains his problem (first visit).
> C gives his solution (take a taxi).
> A introduces the complication to his solution ('But he did not know how...').
> Order: D B C A.

**Q3. Rearrange the sentences:**
*1. A dog stole a piece of meat from a butcher's shop.*
*P. He looked down and saw his own reflection.*
*Q. On his way home, he had to cross a bridge over a stream.*
*R. He thought it was another dog with another piece of meat.*
*S. He barked at the reflection and his meat fell into the stream.*
*6. He lost his meat and had to go home hungry.*
> **Solution:** 1 is given as the starting sentence.
> Q follows 1 (starts his journey).
> P follows Q (looks down from bridge).
> R follows P (interprets reflection).
> S follows R (action taken).
> 6 is the conclusion.
> Order: Q P R S.
