# Training Module 6: Verbal Ability

## 1. Sentence Correction & Grammar

**Q1. Find the error: One of the boys (A) / who always give (B) / the correct answer (C) / is Samuel. (D)**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Break down the sentence structure. The main subject is "One", and the main verb is "is" (in part D). This subject-verb pair is correct.
*   **Step 2:** Analyze the relative clause: "who always give the correct answer".
*   **Step 3:** Apply the IndiaBix grammar rule: In the structure **"One of the + Plural Noun + who/which/that"**, the relative pronoun ("who") refers back to the immediate antecedent (the Plural Noun, which is "boys").
*   **Step 4:** Determine the correct verb for the relative clause. Since "who" refers to the plural "boys", the verb must be plural. The verb "give" is plural.
*   **Final Answer:** The sentence is completely grammatically correct. There is NO ERROR. *(Trick: Many students wrongly change 'give' to 'gives', thinking 'One' is the subject of that verb).*

**Q2. Find the error: The sceneries (A) / of Kashmir (B) / is very charming. (C) / No error (D)**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Analyze the subject noun in part (A): "sceneries".
*   **Step 2:** Apply the uncountable noun rule. Words like *scenery, furniture, information, poetry, luggage* are uncountable nouns in English.
*   **Step 3:** Determine the rule for uncountable nouns. They do not have a plural form (you cannot add 's' or 'ies') and they always take a singular verb.
*   **Step 4:** Correct the sentence. It should be "The scenery of Kashmir is...".
*   **Final Answer:** The error is in part (A).

**Q3. Find the error: I have been (A) / working on this project (B) / since three years. (C) / No error (D)**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Look at the tense used: Present Perfect Continuous ("have been working"). This is correct for an action that started in the past and continues now.
*   **Step 2:** Analyze the time preposition used in part (C): "since".
*   **Step 3:** Apply the Since vs For rule.
    *   **Since** is used for a specific starting *point* in time (e.g., since 2010, since Monday, since morning).
    *   **For** is used for a *duration* or length of time (e.g., for three years, for a month, for two hours).
*   **Step 4:** Correct the sentence. "Three years" is a duration, so it must use "for". It should be "for three years".
*   **Final Answer:** The error is in part (C).

**Q4. Choose the correctly spelt word:**
*A. Ommission*
*B. Omission*
*C. Ommision*
*D. Omision*

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Look at the root word: "Omit".
*   **Step 2:** Apply the spelling rule. When adding the suffix "-ion" to "omit", the 't' changes to double 's', but the 'm' remains single.
*   **Final Answer:** The correct spelling is B. Omission.

**Q5. The antonym of 'OBSCURE' is:**
*A. Pedantic*
*B. Implicit*
*C. Explicit*
*D. Obnoxious*

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Define the given word. "Obscure" means hidden, unclear, or difficult to understand.
*   **Step 2:** Evaluate the options for the opposite meaning (antonym).
    *   *Pedantic:* Overly concerned with formal rules and details.
    *   *Implicit:* Implied though not plainly expressed. (Similar to obscure).
    *   *Explicit:* Stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion.
    *   *Obnoxious:* Extremely unpleasant.
*   **Step 3:** "Explicit" (clear) is the direct opposite of "Obscure" (unclear).
*   **Final Answer:** C. Explicit.

## 2. Para Jumbles

**Q1. Rearrange the sentences to form a coherent paragraph:**
*P. He was so busy with them that he did not get time to eat.*
*Q. Thousands of people came to him and asked different types of questions.*
*R. Swami Vivekananda once stayed in a small village.*
*S. This continued for three days.*

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Find the introductory sentence. Sentence R introduces the main character (Swami Vivekananda) and the setting (a small village). This stands independently. (Start: R)
*   **Step 2:** Find what happened in the village. Sentence Q explains the event that occurred while he was there (people came to him). (Order: R $\rightarrow$ Q)
*   **Step 3:** Look for Pronoun connections. Sentence P starts with "He" and mentions "them". "He" refers to Vivekananda, and "them" refers to the "people" mentioned in Q. Thus, P must immediately follow Q. (Order: R $\rightarrow$ Q $\rightarrow$ P)
*   **Step 4:** Find the concluding statement. Sentence S summarizes the duration of the entire event described in Q and P ("This continued...").
*   **Final Answer:** R - Q - P - S.

**Q2. Rearrange the sentences:**
*A. But he did not know how to find one at that hour.*
*B. It was his first visit to the city and he didn't know where to go.*
*C. Mohan decided to take a taxi.*
*D. The train arrived at the station at midnight.*

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Establish the setting/beginning of the story. Sentence D introduces the starting event (arriving at midnight). (Start: D)
*   **Step 2:** Explain the character's situation. Sentence B explains his predicament upon arriving (first visit, lost). (Order: D $\rightarrow$ B)
*   **Step 3:** Identify the character's proposed solution. Sentence C introduces the specific character "Mohan" (which "he" in sentence B likely refers to, though normally the name comes first, here the narrative flow demands the problem before the specific solution of a taxi). Actually, the arrival triggers the confusion, which triggers the decision. (Order: D $\rightarrow$ B $\rightarrow$ C)
*   **Step 4:** Look for contrasting conjunctions. Sentence A starts with "But", introducing a hurdle to the taxi idea mentioned in C. Thus, A must follow C.
*   **Final Answer:** D - B - C - A.

**Q3. Rearrange the sentences:**
*1. A dog stole a piece of meat from a butcher's shop.*
*P. He looked down and saw his own reflection.*
*Q. On his way home, he had to cross a bridge over a stream.*
*R. He thought it was another dog with another piece of meat.*
*S. He barked at the reflection and his meat fell into the stream.*
*6. He lost his meat and had to go home hungry.*

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** The first sentence (1) and last sentence (6) are fixed. The dog starts with meat and ends up losing it.
*   **Step 2:** Determine the immediate action after stealing. Sentence Q describes his journey home with the meat. (Order: 1 $\rightarrow$ Q)
*   **Step 3:** Determine what happened on the bridge. Sentence P states he looked down (into the stream mentioned in Q). (Order: 1 $\rightarrow$ Q $\rightarrow$ P)
*   **Step 4:** Interpret his reaction to looking down. Sentence R explains his thought process upon seeing the reflection mentioned in P. (Order: 1 $\rightarrow$ Q $\rightarrow$ P $\rightarrow$ R)
*   **Step 5:** Determine the action caused by his thought. Because he thought it was another dog (R), he barked (S). This logically leads directly to the fixed conclusion (6).
*   **Final Answer:** Q - P - R - S.
