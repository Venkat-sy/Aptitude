**Q1. In which year was the percentage of production of Type E vehicles to the total production of vehicles the maximum?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** The question asks to maximize the ratio: $\frac{\text{Type E}}{\text{Total}}$. Calculate this for each year.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate for 1998.
    $\frac{3}{96} = \frac{1}{32} \approx 3.1\%$
*   **Step 3:** Calculate for 1999.
    $\frac{4}{116} = \frac{1}{29} \approx 3.4\%$
*   **Step 4:** Calculate for 2000.
    $\frac{5}{123} \approx 4.06\%$
*   **Step 5:** Calculate for 2001.
    $\frac{6}{139} \approx 4.3\%$
*   **Step 6:** Calculate for 2002.
    $\frac{8}{159} \approx 5.03\%$
*   **Final Answer:** By comparing the percentages, 2002 has the highest percentage (5.03%).

**Q2. The percentage increase in the production of Type B vehicles from 1998 to 2002 is:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Extract the relevant data from the table.
    Production of Type B in 1998 = 34.
    Production of Type B in 2002 = 51.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate the absolute increase.
    $\text{Increase} = 51 - 34 = 17$.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate the percentage increase. The formula is $\left(\frac{\text{Increase}}{\text{Initial Value}}\right) \times 100$.
    $\text{Percentage Increase} = \left(\frac{17}{34}\right) \times 100$.
*   **Step 4:** Simplify.
    $\frac{17}{34} = \frac{1}{2}$.
    $\frac{1}{2} \times 100 = 50\%$.
*   **Final Answer:** 50%.

**Q3. The production of Type C vehicles in 2001 is approximately what percent of the total production of vehicles in 1999?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Extract the relevant data from the table.
    Type C vehicles in 2001 = 40.
    Total production in 1999 = 116.
*   **Step 2:** Set up the percentage calculation. The question is "40 is what percent of 116?".
    $\text{Percentage} = \left(\frac{40}{116}\right) \times 100$.
*   **Step 3:** Simplify the fraction by dividing numerator and denominator by 4.
    $\frac{40}{116} = \frac{10}{29}$.
*   **Step 4:** Calculate the approximate percentage.
    $\frac{10}{29} \times 100 \approx \frac{10}{30} \times 100 = 33.33\%$.
    Since the denominator was 29, the actual answer is slightly higher than 33.33%.
    Exact calculation = $34.48\%$.
*   **Final Answer:** Approximately 34.5%.

**Q4. What is the ratio of the total production of Type A vehicles for the given years to the total production of Type C vehicles for the given years?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Calculate the sum of Type A vehicles across all years.
    $\text{Total A} = 27 + 29 + 25 + 30 + 34 = 145$.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate the sum of Type C vehicles across all years.
    $\text{Total C} = 23 + 31 + 35 + 40 + 45 = 174$.
*   **Step 3:** Set up the ratio.
    Ratio = $145 : 174$.
*   **Step 4:** Simplify the ratio. Try dividing by prime numbers. Both are divisible by 29.
    $145 \div 29 = 5$.
    $174 \div 29 = 6$.
*   **Final Answer:** The ratio is $5 : 6$.

**Q5. In which year was the total production of all types of vehicles exactly equal to the average total production over the given years?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Find the sum of the total production for all 5 years.
    $\text{Total of totals} = 96 + 116 + 123 + 139 + 159 = 633$.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate the average total production.
    $\text{Average} = \frac{\text{Total sum}}{\text{Number of years}}$
    $\text{Average} = \frac{633}{5} = 126.6$.
*   **Step 3:** Compare this average to the total column for each year.
    1998: 96
    1999: 116
    2000: 123
    2001: 139
    2002: 159
*   **Step 4:** Determine the answer. None of the years have a total exactly equal to 126.6. (If the question asks for the "closest" year, it would be 2000. However, "exactly equal" means none).
*   **Final Answer:** None of the given years.
