## 1. Time & Work

**Q1. A can do a work in 15 days and B in 20 days. If they work on it together for 4 days, then the fraction of the work that is left is:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Use the LCM method. Find the LCM of the days (15 and 20) to represent the Total Work.
    $\text{LCM}(15, 20) = 60 \text{ units}$. Let's say there are 60 bricks to lay.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate daily efficiency.
    A's efficiency = $\frac{60}{15} = 4 \text{ units/day}$.
    B's efficiency = $\frac{60}{20} = 3 \text{ units/day}$.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate combined efficiency and work done in 4 days.
    Combined efficiency = $4 + 3 = 7 \text{ units/day}$.
    Work done in 4 days = $7 \times 4 = 28 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 4:** Find the remaining work.
    Remaining = $60 - 28 = 32 \text{ units}$.
*   **Final Answer:** Fraction left = $\frac{32}{60} = \frac{8}{15}$.

**Q2. A is twice as good a workman as B and together they finish a piece of work in 18 days. In how many days will A alone finish the work?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Set up the efficiency ratio.
    A : B = 2 : 1 (This means if B does 1 unit of work a day, A does 2 units).
*   **Step 2:** Find total combined efficiency.
    Total efficiency = $2 + 1 = 3 \text{ units/day}$.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate Total Work based on their combined time.
    Total Work = $\text{Days taken together} \times \text{Combined Efficiency}$
    Total Work = $18 \times 3 = 54 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 4:** Calculate time taken by A alone.
    Time for A = $\frac{\text{Total Work}}{\text{A's Efficiency}} = \frac{54}{2}$
*   **Final Answer:** A takes 27 days.

**Q3. 39 persons can repair a road in 12 days, working 5 hours a day. In how many days will 30 persons, working 6 hours a day, complete the work?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Use the MDH rule: $\frac{M_1 \times D_1 \times H_1}{W_1} = \frac{M_2 \times D_2 \times H_2}{W_2}$
*   **Step 2:** Plug in the values (Work is the same, so $W_1 = W_2 = 1$).
    $39 \times 12 \times 5 = 30 \times D_2 \times 6$
*   **Step 3:** Solve for $D_2$.
    $D_2 = \frac{39 \times 12 \times 5}{30 \times 6}$
    $D_2 = \frac{39 \times 60}{180}$
    $D_2 = \frac{39}{3} = 13$
*   **Final Answer:** 13 days.

**Q4. A and B can do a piece of work in 30 days, while B and C can do the same work in 24 days and C and A in 20 days. They all work together for 10 days when B and C leave. How many days more will A take to finish the work?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Find Total Work using LCM of days (30, 24, 20).
    $\text{LCM}(30, 24, 20) = 120 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate pair efficiencies.
    (A + B) efficiency = $\frac{120}{30} = 4$
    (B + C) efficiency = $\frac{120}{24} = 5$
    (C + A) efficiency = $\frac{120}{20} = 6$
*   **Step 3:** Find combined efficiency of A, B, and C.
    Add all three equations: $2(A + B + C) = 4 + 5 + 6 = 15$
    $(A + B + C) = 7.5$
*   **Step 4:** Find A's individual efficiency.
    $A = (A + B + C) - (B + C) = 7.5 - 5 = 2.5 \text{ units/day}$.
*   **Step 5:** Calculate work done in the first 10 days by all three.
    Work done = $10 \times 7.5 = 75 \text{ units}$.
    Remaining work = $120 - 75 = 45 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 6:** Time taken by A to finish the remaining 45 units.
    Time = $\frac{45}{2.5} = 18$
*   **Final Answer:** 18 days more.

**Q5. A machine P can print one lakh books in 8 hours, machine Q can print the same number of books in 10 hours while machine R can print them in 12 hours. All the machines are started at 9 A.M. while machine P is closed at 11 A.M. and the remaining two machines complete work. Approximately at what time will the work be finished?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Find Total Work using LCM(8, 10, 12).
    $\text{LCM} = 120 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate hourly efficiencies.
    P = $\frac{120}{8} = 15 \text{ units/hr}$
    Q = $\frac{120}{10} = 12 \text{ units/hr}$
    R = $\frac{120}{12} = 10 \text{ units/hr}$
    Combined (P+Q+R) = $15 + 12 + 10 = 37 \text{ units/hr}$.
*   **Step 3:** Work done from 9 AM to 11 AM (2 hours) by all three.
    Work done = $37 \times 2 = 74 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 4:** Calculate remaining work.
    Remaining = $120 - 74 = 46 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 5:** Find time taken by Q and R to finish the remainder.
    Combined efficiency of Q and R = $12 + 10 = 22 \text{ units/hr}$.
    Time = $\frac{46}{22} = 2.09 \text{ hours}$ (approx 2 hours).
*   **Step 6:** Add this time to 11 AM.
    $11 \text{ AM} + 2 \text{ hours} = 1 \text{ P.M.}$
*   **Final Answer:** Approximately 1 P.M.

## 2. Pipes & Cisterns

**Q1. Two pipes A and B can fill a tank in 20 and 30 minutes respectively. If both the pipes are used together, then how long will it take to fill the tank?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** LCM of 20 and 30 is 60 (Total Work).
*   **Step 2:** Calculate minute-by-minute efficiencies.
    A = $\frac{60}{20} = 3 \text{ units/min}$.
    B = $\frac{60}{30} = 2 \text{ units/min}$.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate combined efficiency and time.
    Total efficiency = $3 + 2 = 5 \text{ units/min}$.
    Total time = $\frac{60}{5} = 12 \text{ mins}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 12 minutes.

**Q2. A pump can fill a tank with water in 2 hours. Because of a leak, it took 2(1/3) hours to fill the tank. The leak can drain all the water of the tank in:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Convert mixed fractions. Time with leak = $\frac{7}{3}$ hours.
*   **Step 2:** Find Total Work using LCM of the numerators (2 and 7). Let work = 14 units.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate efficiencies.
    Pump's efficiency = $\frac{14}{2} = 7 \text{ units/hr}$.
    Net efficiency (Pump + Leak) = $\frac{14}{7/3} = 14 \times \frac{3}{7} = 6 \text{ units/hr}$.
*   **Step 4:** Find the Leak's efficiency.
    $\text{Net} = \text{Pump} + \text{Leak} \Rightarrow 6 = 7 + \text{Leak} \Rightarrow \text{Leak} = -1 \text{ units/hr}$.
*   **Step 5:** Time for leak to drain the full tank (14 units).
    Time = $\frac{14}{1} = 14 \text{ hours}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 14 hours.

**Q3. Two pipes A and B can fill a cistern in 37(1/2) minutes and 45 minutes respectively. Both pipes are opened. The cistern will be filled in just half an hour, if the B is turned off after:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Convert 37.5 to fraction = $\frac{75}{2}$.
*   **Step 2:** Let Work = LCM of numerators (75, 45) = 225 units.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate efficiencies.
    A's efficiency = $\frac{225}{75/2} = 6 \text{ units/min}$.
    B's efficiency = $\frac{225}{45} = 5 \text{ units/min}$.
*   **Step 4:** Analyze the timeline. The cistern is filled in exactly 30 minutes. This means Pipe A was running for the entire 30 minutes.
    Work done by A in 30 minutes = $30 \times 6 = 180 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 5:** Calculate the remaining work done by B before it was turned off.
    Remaining work = $225 - 180 = 45 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 6:** Time taken by B to do 45 units.
    Time = $\frac{45}{5} = 9 \text{ minutes}$.
*   **Final Answer:** B should be turned off after 9 minutes.

**Q4. Three pipes A, B and C can fill a tank from empty to full in 30 minutes, 20 minutes, and 10 minutes respectively. When the tank is empty, all the three pipes are opened. A, B and C discharge chemical solutions P,Q and R respectively. What is the proportion of solution R in the liquid in the tank after 3 minutes?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Note that proportion doesn't depend on time (3 minutes is a distractor). The proportion depends entirely on their flow rates (efficiencies).
*   **Step 2:** Find total work LCM(30, 20, 10) = 60 units.
*   **Step 3:** Efficiencies (Flow rates):
    A (Solution P) = $\frac{60}{30} = 2$
    B (Solution Q) = $\frac{60}{20} = 3$
    C (Solution R) = $\frac{60}{10} = 6$
*   **Step 4:** Calculate the proportion of Solution R.
    Total liquid flowing in per minute = $2 + 3 + 6 = 11$ parts.
    Amount of R flowing in per minute = $6$ parts.
    Proportion of R = $\frac{6}{11}$.
*   **Final Answer:** $\frac{6}{11}$.

**Q5. A water tank is two-fifth full. Pipe A can fill a tank in 10 minutes and pipe B can empty it in 6 minutes. If both the pipes are open, how long will it take to empty or fill the tank completely?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Compare speeds. Emptying pipe B (6 mins) is faster than filling pipe A (10 mins). Therefore, the tank will eventually **empty**.
*   **Step 2:** Let Total Capacity = LCM(10, 6) = 30 units.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate efficiencies.
    A = $+3 \text{ units/min}$
    B = $-5 \text{ units/min}$
    Net efficiency = $3 - 5 = -2 \text{ units/min}$ (emptying).
*   **Step 4:** The tank is $\frac{2}{5}$ full. Calculate the volume of water currently inside.
    Volume = $\frac{2}{5} \times 30 = 12 \text{ units}$.
*   **Step 5:** Calculate time to empty 12 units at a net rate of 2 units/min.
    Time = $\frac{12}{2} = 6 \text{ minutes}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 6 minutes to empty.

## 3. Time, Speed & Distance

**Q1. An aeroplane covers a certain distance at a speed of 240 kmph in 5 hours. To cover the same distance in 1(2/3) hours, it must travel at a speed of:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Calculate the Total Distance.
    $\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$
    $\text{Distance} = 240 \times 5 = 1200 \text{ km}$.
*   **Step 2:** Convert mixed fraction to improper fraction.
    New Time = $1\frac{2}{3} = \frac{5}{3} \text{ hours}$.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate the required speed.
    $\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{1200}{5/3} = 1200 \times \frac{3}{5} = 240 \times 3 = 720 \text{ kmph}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 720 kmph.

**Q2. A man performs 2/15 of the total journey by rail, 9/20 by bus and the remaining 10 km on cycle. His total journey is:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Calculate the total fraction of the journey covered by rail and bus.
    $\text{Fraction covered} = \frac{2}{15} + \frac{9}{20}$
    LCM of 15 and 20 is 60.
    $\frac{8}{60} + \frac{27}{60} = \frac{35}{60} = \frac{7}{12}$.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate the remaining fraction of the journey.
    $\text{Remaining fraction} = 1 - \frac{7}{12} = \frac{5}{12}$.
*   **Step 3:** Set up the equation for total journey ($x$).
    $\frac{5}{12} \text{ of } x = 10 \text{ km}$.
    $x = \frac{10 \times 12}{5} = 24 \text{ km}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 24 km.

**Q3. Excluding stoppages, the speed of a bus is 54 kmph and including stoppages, it is 45 kmph. For how many minutes does the bus stop per hour?**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Use the standard formula for this specific IndiaBix question.
    $\text{Stoppage Time (hrs)} = \frac{\text{Difference in Speeds}}{\text{Speed without Stoppage}}$
*   **Step 2:** Plug in the values.
    $\text{Difference} = 54 - 45 = 9 \text{ kmph}$.
    $\text{Time} = \frac{9}{54} \text{ hours}$.
*   **Step 3:** Convert to minutes.
    $\text{Time in mins} = \left(\frac{1}{6}\right) \times 60 = 10 \text{ minutes}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 10 minutes.

**Q4. If a person walks at 14 km/hr instead of 10 km/hr, he would have walked 20 km more. The actual distance travelled by him is:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Since the time spent walking is constant in both scenarios, Distance is directly proportional to Speed.
    Let the actual distance be $x$.
    Time = $\frac{x}{10}$
*   **Step 2:** Setup the equation for the hypothetical scenario.
    Time = $\frac{(x + 20)}{14}$
*   **Step 3:** Equate the times.
    $\frac{x}{10} = \frac{x + 20}{14}$
*   **Step 4:** Cross multiply and solve.
    $14x = 10x + 200$
    $4x = 200$
    $x = 50 \text{ km}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 50 km.

**Q5. A car traveling with 5/7 of its actual speed covers 42 km in 1 hr 40 min 48 sec. Find the actual speed of the car.**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Convert the time entirely into hours.
    $1 \text{ hr } 40 \text{ min } 48 \text{ sec} = 1 + \frac{40}{60} + \frac{48}{3600}$
    $= 1 + \frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{75}$
    $= \frac{75 + 50 + 1}{75} = \frac{126}{75} \text{ hours}$.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate the new, reduced speed.
    $\text{New Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{42}{126/75} = \frac{42 \times 75}{126}$
    $= \frac{75}{3} = 25 \text{ km/hr}$.
*   **Step 3:** Find actual speed. The new speed is 5/7 of the actual speed.
    $\frac{5}{7} \times \text{Actual} = 25$
    $\text{Actual} = \frac{25 \times 7}{5} = 35 \text{ km/hr}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 35 km/hr.

## 4. Trains, Boats & Streams

**Q1. Two trains running in opposite directions cross a man standing on the platform in 27 seconds and 17 seconds respectively and they cross each other in 23 seconds. The ratio of their speeds is:**

**Solution:**
*   **Trick Approach (Alligation):**
    You can use the rule of alligation on the times!
    Train 1 time to cross man: 27
    Train 2 time to cross man: 17
    Time to cross each other: 23 (Middle value)
    *   Ratio = $(23 - 17) : (27 - 23)$
    *   Ratio = $6 : 4$
    *   Simplify = $3 : 2$
*   **Final Answer:** 3:2.

**Q2. A train 125 m long passes a man, running at 5 km/hr in the same direction in which the train is going, in 10 seconds. The speed of the train is:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Let the speed of the train be $S$ km/hr.
    Since they are moving in the same direction, Relative Speed = $(S - 5) \text{ km/hr}$.
*   **Step 2:** Convert Relative Speed to m/s.
    Relative Speed = $(S - 5) \times \frac{5}{18} \text{ m/s}$.
*   **Step 3:** Use the formula: $\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$.
    Distance here is just the train's length = 125m.
    $125 = \left[(S - 5) \times \frac{5}{18}\right] \times 10$
*   **Step 4:** Solve for S.
    $125 = (S - 5) \times \frac{50}{18}$
    $S - 5 = 125 \times \frac{18}{50}$
    $S - 5 = \frac{5 \times 18}{2} = 45$
    $S = 50 \text{ km/hr}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 50 km/hr.

**Q3. A boat can travel with a speed of 13 km/hr in still water. If the speed of the stream is 4 km/hr, find the time taken by the boat to go 68 km downstream.**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Calculate Downstream speed.
    $\text{Downstream Speed} = \text{Speed in still water} + \text{Speed of stream}$
    $= 13 + 4 = 17 \text{ km/hr}$.
*   **Step 2:** Calculate Time.
    $\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}$
    $= \frac{68}{17} = 4 \text{ hours}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 4 hours.

**Q4. A man's speed with the current is 15 km/hr and the speed of the current is 2.5 km/hr. The man's speed against the current is:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Break down "speed with the current" (Downstream).
    $\text{Downstream} = \text{Man's speed in still water (u)} + \text{Current (v)} = 15$.
*   **Step 2:** We know $v = 2.5$.
    $u + 2.5 = 15 \Rightarrow u = 12.5 \text{ km/hr}$.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate "speed against the current" (Upstream).
    $\text{Upstream} = u - v$
    $= 12.5 - 2.5 = 10 \text{ km/hr}$.
*   **Final Answer:** 10 km/hr.

**Q5. A motorboat, whose speed in 15 km/hr in still water goes 30 km downstream and comes back in a total of 4 hours 30 minutes. The speed of the stream (in km/hr) is:**

**Solution:**
*   **Step 1:** Let the speed of the stream be $v$.
*   **Step 2:** Setup the total time equation.
    Time Downstream + Time Upstream = Total Time.
    $\frac{30}{15 + v} + \frac{30}{15 - v} = 4.5 \text{ hours}$.
*   **Step 3:** Solve by testing options (usually 4, 5, 6 on IndiaBix).
    Let's test $v = 5$:
    $\frac{30}{15 + 5} + \frac{30}{15 - 5} = \frac{30}{20} + \frac{30}{10} = 1.5 + 3 = 4.5$.
    This perfectly matches our total time!
*   **Final Answer:** 5 km/hr.
