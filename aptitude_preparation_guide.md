# IndiaBix-Style Aptitude & Reasoning Preparation Guide

This guide is heavily tailored to match the patterns, formulas, and classic questions found on platforms like **IndiaBix**. It provides the essential formulas, shortcut tricks, and sample questions to crack the PAT.

## 1. Quantitative Aptitude

### A. Arithmetic Fundamentals

#### 1. Percentages & Profit/Loss
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   **Net Percentage Change:** If a value is increased by $x\%$ and then decreased by $y\%$, net change $= x - y - \frac{xy}{100}$.
*   **False Weight (Profit/Loss):** $\text{Gain } \% = \left[ \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{True Value} - \text{Error}} \right] \times 100\%$

> **IndiaBix Classic Question:** A dishonest dealer professes to sell his goods at cost price, but he uses a weight of 960 grams for the 1 kg weight. Find his gain percent.
> **Solution (Trick):** 
> $\text{Gain } \% = \left[ \frac{1000 - 960}{960} \right] \times 100\% = \frac{40}{960} \times 100\% = \frac{100}{24}\% = 4.16\% \text{ or } 4\frac{1}{6}\%$.

#### 2. Ratio & Proportion
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   **Fourth Proportional:** If $a:b :: c:x$, then $x = \frac{bc}{a}$.
*   **Mean Proportional:** Between $a$ and $b$ is $\sqrt{ab}$.

#### 3. Simple & Compound Interest
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   **Population Growth/Depreciation:** $P\left(1 \pm \frac{R}{100}\right)^n$
*   **Difference between CI and SI for 2 years:** $D = P \left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2$

> **IndiaBix Classic Question:** The difference between simple and compound interests compounded annually on a certain sum of money for 2 years at 4% per annum is Re. 1. The sum is:
> **Solution (Trick):** 
> $1 = P \times \left(\frac{4}{100}\right)^2 \Rightarrow 1 = P \times \left(\frac{1}{25}\right)^2 \Rightarrow P = 625$.

#### 4. Averages & Ages
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   Average speed for equal distances at speed $x$ and $y$ is $\frac{2xy}{x+y}$.

> **IndiaBix Classic Question:** The sum of the present ages of a father and his son is 60 years. Six years ago, father's age was five times the age of the son. After 6 years, son's age will be:
> **Solution:**
> Let present ages be $F$ and $S$. $F + S = 60 \Rightarrow F = 60 - S$.
> 6 years ago: $(F - 6) = 5(S - 6)$
> Substitute $F$: $(60 - S - 6) = 5S - 30 \Rightarrow 54 - S = 5S - 30 \Rightarrow 6S = 84 \Rightarrow S = 14$.
> Son's age after 6 years $= 14 + 6 = 20$ years.

### B. Work, Speed & Measurement

#### 1. Time & Work (MDH Rule)
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   **The MDH Formula:** $\frac{M_1 \times D_1 \times H_1}{W_1} = \frac{M_2 \times D_2 \times H_2}{W_2}$ (Men $\times$ Days $\times$ Hours / Work)
*   If A does work in $x$ days, B in $y$ days, together they take $\frac{xy}{x+y}$ days.

> **IndiaBix Classic Question:** 39 persons can repair a road in 12 days, working 5 hours a day. In how many days will 30 persons, working 6 hours a day, complete the work?
> **Solution (Trick):**
> $\frac{39 \times 12 \times 5}{1} = \frac{30 \times D_2 \times 6}{1}$
> $D_2 = \frac{39 \times 12 \times 5}{30 \times 6} = 13$ days.

#### 2. Pipes & Cisterns
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   If a pipe fills a tank in $x$ hours and another empties it in $y$ hours (where $y > x$), net part filled in 1 hour $= \frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{y}$. Total time $= \frac{xy}{y-x}$.

#### 3. Trains
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   Time taken by a train of length $L$ moving at speed $S$ to cross a stationary object of length $P$ (platform/bridge) $= \frac{L + P}{S}$.
*   Time taken by two trains of lengths $L_1$ and $L_2$ moving in opposite directions at speeds $S_1$ and $S_2$ to cross each other $= \frac{L_1 + L_2}{S_1 + S_2}$.

> **IndiaBix Classic Question:** A train 125 m long passes a man, running at 5 km/hr in the same direction in which the train is going, in 10 seconds. The speed of the train is:
> **Solution:**
> Let speed of train be $S$ km/hr. Relative speed $= (S - 5)$ km/hr $= (S - 5) \times \frac{5}{18}$ m/s.
> Distance $= 125$ m. Time $= 10$ s.
> $(S - 5) \times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{125}{10} \Rightarrow S - 5 = 12.5 \times \frac{18}{5} = 45 \Rightarrow S = 50$ km/hr.

#### 4. Boats & Streams
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   Let speed of boat in still water be $u$ and speed of stream be $v$.
*   Downstream speed ($D$) $= u + v$
*   Upstream speed ($U$) $= u - v$
*   $u = \frac{D + U}{2}$ and $v = \frac{D - U}{2}$

### C. Probability & Counting Techniques

#### 1. Permutation & Combination
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   Always distinguish between "Arrangement" (Permutation, $^nP_r$) and "Selection" (Combination, $^nC_r$).

> **IndiaBix Classic Question:** Out of 7 consonants and 4 vowels, how many words of 3 consonants and 2 vowels can be formed?
> **Solution:**
> Number of ways of selecting $= (^7C_3 \times ^4C_2) = 35 \times 6 = 210$.
> Total letters selected $= 5$. These can be arranged in $5!$ ways $= 120$.
> Total words $= 210 \times 120 = 25200$.

## 2. Logical Reasoning

### A. Analytical Reasoning

#### 1. Number Series
**IndiaBix Pattern Tricks:**
*   Look for common IndiaBix patterns: Prime numbers, $n^2-1$, $n^3+1$, alternating series, or multiplied by 0.5, 1.5, 2.5...

> **IndiaBix Classic Question:** Look at this series: 7, 10, 8, 11, 9, 12, ... What number should come next?
> **Solution:** This is a simple alternating addition and subtraction series. In the first pattern, 3 is added; in the second, 2 is subtracted. ($7+3=10, 10-2=8, 8+3=11, 11-2=9, 9+3=12, 12-2=10$). Answer is 10.

#### 2. Blood Relations
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   Break down statements like "My father's only son" into "Me" (if speaker is male). Always work backward from the end of the sentence.

#### 3. Direction Sense
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks:**
*   Always draw a small diagram. Remember that after consecutive left and right turns, they cancel each other out.

### B. Decision Making & Logic

#### 1. Syllogisms
**IndiaBix Formula Tricks (The Tick/Cross Method or Venn):**
*   "All A are B" -> Draw A inside B.
*   "Some A are B" -> Overlap A and B.
*   "No A is B" -> Keep A and B entirely separate.

> **IndiaBix Classic Question:** 
> Statements: All branches are flowers. All flowers are leaves.
> Conclusions: 
> 1. All branches are leaves. 
> 2. All leaves are branches.
> 3. All flowers are branches. 
> 4. Some leaves are branches.
> **Solution:** 
> Draw Branches inside Flowers, and Flowers inside Leaves.
> Since Branches are entirely inside Leaves, (1) is true.
> Since Leaves is the largest circle, (4) is true (the part where branches are).
> Answer: Only 1 and 4 follow.

## 3. Data Interpretation

**IndiaBix Data Analysis Tricks:**
*   **Line/Bar Graphs:** In IndiaBix, look out for questions asking for "percentage increase/decrease from previous year". 
    *   Formula: $\frac{\text{Current Year} - \text{Previous Year}}{\text{Previous Year}} \times 100$.
*   **Pie Charts:** Total angle is $360^\circ$. $1\% = 3.6^\circ$. To convert an angle to percentage, divide by $3.6$.

## 4. Verbal Ability

### A. Grammar & Placement Communication
**IndiaBix Common Error Rules:**
*   "One of the" is always followed by a **plural noun** and a **singular verb**. (e.g., "One of the *boys is* missing").
*   Words like 'furniture', 'information', 'poetry', 'scenery' are uncountable and always take singular verbs.

> **IndiaBix Classic Error Correction:** "I am playing cricket for more than three years."
> **Correction:** Because there is a duration ("for more than three years"), it should be Present Perfect Continuous: "I **have been** playing..."

### B. Reading Comprehension & Para Jumbles
**IndiaBix Para Jumbles Tricks:**
*   Look for sentences starting with 'But', 'However', 'Therefore' - these can never be the opening sentences.
*   Find Noun-Pronoun relationships. If sentence A mentions "Mr. Sharma" and sentence C mentions "He", A will come before C.
*   Chronological order: Time indicators (Initially, later, finally) dictate the flow.
